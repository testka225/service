# Service Desk - Organizational Structure

## Overview

The Service Desk operates as a global, distributed team providing 24/7 support across multiple time zones. This document outlines the organizational structure, roles, responsibilities, and reporting relationships.

## Organizational Model

### Follow-the-Sun Support Model

The Service Desk operates on a follow-the-sun model with three primary regional hubs:

1. **Americas Hub** (Coverage: GMT-8 to GMT-5)
2. **EMEA Hub** (Coverage: GMT to GMT+3)
3. **APAC Hub** (Coverage: GMT+8 to GMT+11)

This ensures continuous 24/7 coverage with seamless handoffs between regions.

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Service Desk Director
**Reports to:** Chief Information Officer (CIO)

**Responsibilities:**
- Overall strategic direction of global Service Desk operations
- Budget management and resource allocation
- Stakeholder relationship management
- Service delivery accountability
- Performance monitoring and reporting
- Change and transformation leadership

**Key Metrics:**
- Overall customer satisfaction
- SLA compliance across all regions
- Team engagement and retention
- Budget adherence

---

### Service Desk Manager (Regional)
**Reports to:** Service Desk Director

**Responsibilities:**
- Day-to-day management of regional operations
- Team performance management
- Local stakeholder engagement
- Resource planning and scheduling
- Escalation management
- Regional compliance and policy adherence
- Coordination with other regions for handoffs

**Key Metrics:**
- Regional customer satisfaction
- Regional SLA compliance
- Team productivity and utilization
- First contact resolution rate

---

### Team Lead - L1 Support
**Reports to:** Service Desk Manager (Regional)

- Real-time issue escalation

**Key Metrics:**
- Team response times
- Queue management efficiency

---

### Team Lead - L2 Support
**Reports to:** Service Desk Manager (Regional)

**Responsibilities:**
- Advanced technical issue resolution

- Escalation resolution rate

### Service Desk Analyst (L1)
**Reports to:** Team Lead - L1 Support

**Responsibilities:**
- First point of contact for all support requests
- Initial triage and categorization of incidents
- Password resets and basic account management
- Basic troubleshooting (hardware, software, network)
- Ticket documentation and tracking
- Escalation to L2 when needed
- Following standard operating procedures
- Customer communication and updates

**Skills Required:**
- Strong customer service orientation
- Basic technical troubleshooting
- Clear communication skills
- Multi-tasking ability
- Patience and empathy

**Key Metrics:**
- First contact resolution rate
- Average handle time
- Customer satisfaction scores
- Ticket quality and documentation

---

### Senior Service Desk Analyst (L2)

## Communication and Collaboration

### Regular Meetings

**Daily:**
- Shift handoff meetings (15 minutes)
- Team standup (15 minutes per shift)

**Weekly:**
- Regional team meetings (1 hour)
- L1/L2 coordination meetings (30 minutes)
- Knowledge sharing sessions (30 minutes)

**Monthly:**
- Global all-hands meeting (1 hour)
- Regional performance reviews (1 hour)
- Service improvement reviews (1 hour)

**Quarterly:**
- Strategic planning sessions
- Comprehensive performance reviews
- Team engagement activities

## Escalation Paths

### Technical Escalation
1. L1 Service Desk Analyst
2. L2 Senior Service Desk Analyst
3. Team Lead - L2 Support
4. Specialized IT Teams (Network, Security, Applications)
5. Vendor Support (as needed)

### Management Escalation
1. Team Lead
2. Service Desk Manager (Regional)
3. Service Desk Director
4. CIO (for critical business impact)

### Manufacturing Critical Issues
1. Manufacturing Support Specialist (immediate)
2. Service Desk Manager (notification)
3. Production Management (parallel notification)
4. Service Desk Director (for extended impact)
